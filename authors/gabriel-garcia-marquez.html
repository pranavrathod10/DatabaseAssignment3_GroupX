Gabriel Garcia Marquez - Author

Gabriel Garcia Marquez was a Colombian author and journalist best known for his novels, including "One Hundred Years of Solitude" and "Love in the Time of Cholera."

Biography

Gabriel Garcia Marquez was born in Aracataca, Colombia in 1927. He studied at the National University of Colombia and began his career as a journalist. In 1967, he published "One Hundred Years of Solitude," which went on to become one of the most celebrated novels of the 20th century. He won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982. He died in 2014 at the age of 87.

Works

  • Love in the Time of Cholera
  • The Autumn of the Patriarch
  • Chronicle of a Death Foretold
  • Memories of My Melancholy Whores
