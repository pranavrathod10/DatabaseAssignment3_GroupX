James Baldwin - Author

James Baldwin was an American writer and social critic best known for his essays and novels exploring issues of race and sexuality in the United States.

Biography

James Baldwin was born on August 2, 1924 in Harlem, New York. He grew up in poverty and spent much of his childhood caring for his younger siblings. Baldwin was an avid reader and began writing as a teenager. In 1948, he moved to France where he found greater acceptance as a black writer than he did in the United States. Baldwin returned to the U.S. in 1957 to become more involved in the Civil Rights Movement. He died on December 1, 1987.
